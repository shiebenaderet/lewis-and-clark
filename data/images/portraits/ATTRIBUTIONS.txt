Lewis & Clark Expedition - Portrait Image Attributions
======================================================

All portrait images are public domain works sourced from Wikimedia Commons.
To download the actual images, run: bash download-portraits.sh

5. york-portrait.jpg
   Title:    York
   Artist:   Charles Marion Russell (1864-1926)
   Date:     1908
   Medium:   Watercolor
   Note:     No contemporary portrait of York exists. This painting by C.M. Russell
             depicts York meeting members of the Hidatsa tribe during the expedition.
             York was an enslaved man who was the only African-American member of
             the Lewis and Clark Expedition (1804-1806).
   License:  Public domain (artist died 1926; work created before 1928)
   Commons:  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Charles_M_Russell_York_1908.jpg
   Download: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/14/Charles_M_Russell_York_1908.jpg

7. jean-baptiste-portrait.jpg
   Title:    'Baptiste', a Native American
   Source:   Wellcome Library, London
   Note:     Historical portrait; attribution to Jean Baptiste Charbonneau is
             not certain. Jean Baptiste ("Pomp") was Sacagawea's son, born
             during the expedition in 1805.
   License:  CC-BY 4.0 (Wellcome Library)
   Commons:  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:%27Baptiste%27,_a_Native_American_Wellcome_L0046606.jpg
   Download: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a1/%27Baptiste%27%2C_a_Native_American_Wellcome_L0046606.jpg

8. floyd-monument.jpg
   Title:    Sergeant Floyd Monument
   Location: Floyd's Bluff, Sioux City, Iowa
   Note:     No portrait of Sgt. Charles Floyd exists. This 100-foot sandstone
             obelisk (1901) was the first National Historic Landmark (1960).
             Floyd was the only expedition member to die during the journey
             (August 20, 1804, likely of a ruptured appendix).
   License:  CC-BY-SA 4.0
   Commons:  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Sgt_Floyd_Monument_PA140783.JPG
   Download: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c6/Sgt_Floyd_Monument_PA140783.JPG

9. drouillard-portrait (NO IMAGE AVAILABLE)
   Note:     No known portrait of George Drouillard exists. Drouillard (c. 1773-1810)
             was of French-Canadian and Shawnee descent, served as the expedition's
             primary interpreter, hunter, and scout. Lewis called him "the most
             important person on the expedition, after Lewis and Clark themselves."

10. cameahwait-portrait (NO IMAGE AVAILABLE)
    Note:     No known standalone portrait of Chief Cameahwait exists.
              Cameahwait was the chief of the Lemhi Shoshone and Sacagawea's brother.
              He appears in Edgar Samuel Paxson's murals at the Montana State Capitol,
              but no standalone portrait file is available on Wikimedia Commons.
